> Stories from Game Producer Yoshiki Okamoto: The "popopo" Mobile App

> Writer: [Michael B. Syson](https://www.linkedin.com/in/michaelsyson/)

> Publication Date: 2026-07-07

## --

Dear friends,

Today let me share with you Mr. Yoshiki Okamoto's lecture about "popopo," a mobile app that he continues to use while also launching LIVE videos about it.

Okamoto-san says that there are people who wonder if the app's service would end soon, and he explains the reason, saying that it's because it seems that there aren't a lot of users who take advantage of the opportunity to speak while using a character that they choose instead of showing their own face on the camera, such that, as a result, it doesn't make money.

Further, Okamoto-san points out that when a studio spends millions of yen for a project and only spends without earning money each month, of course, it has to find out what the problem is.

Okamoto-san reveals that in his own group of colleagues in their company space called clubhouse, they very much enjoy this app and its service. However, he thinks that, in the end, there aren't a lot of people who are shy about showing their faces, and would instead choose a toy like a teddy bear when they're talking.

Finally, Okamoto-san concludes that "popopo" has a monetization problem, mentioning that it's a case similar to Unity. More specifically, users only try it, and don't want to go back and use it further. So the in-app purchase function that lets users give money to the studio, seems to him, isn't yet necessary.

First, Okamoto-san explains that he's returned to Malaysia after being in Japan for five weeks due to his trip in the ASUKA III cruise as well as his work in Shibuya. He also feels that he's again going to have to get used to living in Malaysia, informing us that it actually just rained a lot a while ago.

Next, Okamoto-san recounts that in the 1965s, watch company Seiko was able to release a line of luxury watches that could sell together with Swiss watches. Because of this, people like himself began to want these watches, and, in fact, Okamoto-san collects them and puts them in his collection. Furthermore, he wears one that for almost a year he hasn't taken off at all even when he takes a bath or sleeps.

In continuation, among the models of the new Grand Seiko watch, there's one called "White Gold" that costs 8.8 million with a value that is projected to rise by 3.85 million yen. Okamoto-san admits that, in the beginning, he didn't want to buy it, but after hearing about the projection that it'll be valued more by 3.85 million yen, he's decided to buy one.

Okamoto-san also explains that if he has to choose either watch or car, he'd choose the former, because the cars in Malaysia cost more than double of those in Japan due to tariffs. Besides, the roads sometimes aren't suitable for luxury cars. Thus, he reveals that his watches and house are the most profitable for him at the moment.

Finally, Okamoto-san shares with us the reason why the lineup of Grand Seiko watches is expected to cost so much more. He says that it's because of Mr. Shohei Ohtani, the baseball athlete who's very popular right now. According to reports, he's given Grand Seiko watches to his Dodgers teammates and staff members last season. Okamoto-san also recounts that the Grand Seiko model that Ohtani-san wears isn't anymore sold in the market, even in the second-hand market. As a result, his watch now costs quite a lot of money.

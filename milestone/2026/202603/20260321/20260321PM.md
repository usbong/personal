First, Okamoto-san explains that since 30 years ago the prices of goods hadn't changed, so he thinks that there are young people who still haven't experienced this situation until now. Still, in his case, Okamoto-san reveals that he has, in fact, experienced these changes throughout his whole life.

Furthermore, although owning 100 million yen originally could give a person 800,000 yen every year, this isn't anymore the case these days. So, given that each year, prices can be expected to go up 3%, Okamoto-san advises three remedies.

One is to lower everyday expenses. Although prices go up, salaries don't go up along with this change, so this remedy is something that Okamoto-san recommends.

Next is to increase our income by moving to another job or improving our abilities.

Finally, the third remedy is to invest in the stock market with the understanding that an amount of 100 million yen will only give us a little over 400,000 yen per year.
